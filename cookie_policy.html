2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, personalise your experience, and provide information to the website owners.

Similar technologies include web beacons, pixel tags, local storage, and session storage. Where we refer to "cookies" in this policy, we mean all such tracking technologies unless stated otherwise.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies – Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies – Remain on your device for a defined period or until manually deleted.
  • First-party cookies – Set directly by domain.
  • Third-party cookies – Set by external services operating on our pages.

6. Cookie Retention Periods

The retention period for each cookie varies depending on its purpose:

  • Session cookies: Deleted automatically when you close your browser.
  • Short-term persistent cookies: Retained for between 1 minute and 30 days.
  • Medium-term persistent cookies: Retained for 3 to 6 months.
  • Long-term persistent cookies: Retained for up to 24 months.

Specific retention periods for each cookie are detailed in the tables in Section 3 above. We periodically review the cookies in use to ensure retention periods remain appropriate and proportionate to their purpose.

7. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing and controlling cookies:

When you first visit domain.com, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner allowing you to accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or customise your preferences by category. You can update your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer of any page.

7.2 Browser Controls

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • View cookies stored on your device
  • Delete individual or all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies (note: this may affect site functionality)
  • Set preferences for specific websites

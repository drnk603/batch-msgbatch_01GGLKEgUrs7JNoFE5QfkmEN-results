Your GDPR Data Subject Rights

At Velour Noir, we are committed to protecting your personal data and upholding your rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable ePrivacy legislation. This page explains what rights you have, how to exercise them, and how we will respond to your requests.

Last Updated:

Scope & Legal Basis

Velour Noir (operating at domain.com) acts as a data controller for the personal data you provide when using our website, purchasing products, or communicating with us. We process your data in compliance with the GDPR (EU) 2016/679 and the Russian Federal Law on Personal Data (No. 152-FZ) where applicable.

You have a number of important rights under GDPR in connection with the personal data we hold about you. These rights are described in full below. You may exercise any of these rights at any time by contacting us using the details provided in this document.

Your Rights Under GDPR

We take your privacy seriously. Below is a clear explanation of each right you hold as a data subject.

Right of Access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you. This allows you to verify that we are processing your data lawfully. We will provide this information free of charge, typically within 30 days of your request.

Right to Rectification

If any data we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request that we correct or complete it without undue delay. We will act on such requests promptly and notify any third parties who received the inaccurate data where appropriate.

Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")

You may request that we delete your personal data in certain circumstances — for example, if the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or if you withdraw your consent. Some data may be retained for legal obligations even after erasure requests.

Right to Restriction of Processing

In certain situations, you have the right to ask us to restrict how we process your data. For example, if you contest the accuracy of the data or if processing is unlawful but you prefer restriction over erasure. During restriction, we may store but not actively use your data.

Right to Data Portability

Where we process your data based on your consent or for the performance of a contract, and the processing is carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format and to transmit it to another controller.

Right to Object

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data where we rely on legitimate interests as the legal basis. You may also object at any time to processing for direct marketing purposes. Upon objection to direct marketing, we will cease processing your data for those purposes immediately.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we process your data based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal. You can withdraw consent by contacting us or using the unsubscribe mechanism in our communications.

Right Regarding Automated Decision-Making

You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing — including profiling — which produces legal or similarly significant effects. You may request human review of any automated decisions that affect you significantly.

How to Submit a Data Request

To exercise any of your rights described above, please contact our Data Protection team using one of the following methods. We will acknowledge your request within 72 hours and provide a full response within 30 calendar days. In complex cases, this period may be extended by up to an additional 60 days, and we will notify you of any such extension.

01

Contact Us by Email

Send your request to requests@domain.com. Include your full name, the right you wish to exercise, and sufficient information for us to identify you as a data subject.

02

Use Our Contact Form

Visit our Contact page and submit your request through the dedicated form. Select “GDPR / Privacy Request” as the subject of your enquiry.

03

Identity Verification

We may need to verify your identity before processing your request to ensure we protect your data and do not disclose it to unauthorised parties. We will not charge a fee for reasonable requests.

04

Response Timeline

We will respond to all valid data subject requests within 30 calendar days. If an extension is required, we will notify you in writing and explain the reason for the delay.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe that we have not handled your personal data in compliance with applicable data protection law, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a competent supervisory authority.

In Russia, the competent supervisory authority for data protection is:

Roskomnadzor (Federal Service for Supervision in the Sphere of Telecom, Information Technologies and Mass Communications)

Website: rkn.gov.ru

You may also file a complaint with the supervisory authority of the EU member state in which you reside or work, if applicable.

GDPR & ePrivacy Compliance

Velour Noir is committed to full compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU) 2016/679 and the ePrivacy Directive (Directive 2002/58/EC). Our data processing practices are built on the following principles:

  • Lawfulness, Fairness & Transparency: We process data only where we have a valid legal basis and always communicate our practices openly.
  • Purpose Limitation: We collect data only for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and do not process it in ways incompatible with those purposes.
  • Data Minimisation: We ensure that data collected is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary.
  • Accuracy: We take reasonable steps to keep personal data accurate and up to date.
  • Storage Limitation: We retain data only as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law.
  • Integrity & Confidentiality: We implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect personal data.
  • Accountability: We are responsible for demonstrating compliance with all the above principles.

For more information, please also review our Privacy Policy, Cookie Policy, and Terms of Service.
